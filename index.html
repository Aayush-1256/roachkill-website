FAQ

Common questions

Is it safe to use in a kitchen with children and pets at home?
RoachKill is designed for targeted placement inside cracks, hinges and concealed points that children and pets can't reach — unlike sprays, there's no airborne mist or surface residue. As with any household insecticide, read the label before use, apply only at inaccessible points, wash hands after application and store the syringe away from children.
How fast does it work?
Most households see a clear reduction in sightings within 48–72 hours. Full colony elimination typically takes one to two weeks depending on the infestation. Heavy infestations may need a second application after two weeks.
Where exactly should I apply the dots?
Under the sink and around drain pipes, behind the refrigerator, inside cabinet hinges and corners, behind the gas stove, under wash basins, and along skirting cracks — pea-sized dots every one to two feet in active areas. Use the interactive map above; a placement guide is also printed inside every pack.
Why doesn't it kill instantly like a spray?
The delay is the feature. An instant kill stops at one roach. RoachKill's slow-acting formula lets the feeding roach return to the nest and transfer the bait to the rest of the colony — which is how you eliminate the source, not just the symptom.
Is RoachKill approved by the government?
RoachKill is manufactured in a licensed facility and registered as required under the Insecticides Act, 1968. The registration number, manufacturing licence number, directions for use, cautions and first-aid information are printed on every pack. Always read the label and leaflet before use.
